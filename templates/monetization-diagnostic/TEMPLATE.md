# Monetization Diagnostic for AI Tools

**DGAF Framework · T-EH-03 · v1.0.0**

> A root-cause diagnostic for AI tool creators with traffic but no revenue. Distinguishes demand problems from packaging problems from distribution problems — most creators misdiagnose which one they have.

---

## The Diagnostic Premise

When an AI tool generates significant traffic but no revenue, the failure is almost never demand. It's almost always one of three things:

**Type A — Packaging Gap:** The paid offer exists but isn't legible to the buyer. They don't understand what they're buying.

**Type B — CTA Gap:** The paid offer exists but isn't visible to the buyer at the right moment. They never see it.

**Type C — Tier Mismatch:** The paid offer is visible but priced or scoped wrong for the audience that's actually showing up.

This diagnostic identifies which type you have and gives you the fix.

---

## Step 1: Confirm It's Not Demand

Before diagnosing packaging or CTA, confirm there's genuine interest.

- [ ] Your free content has been viewed 1,000+ times
- [ ] At least one person has asked a question, left a comment, or sent a message
- [ ] You've received at least one "how much is this?" or "is there a paid version?"
- [ ] Your topic has paying buyers on comparable platforms (search for similar paid offerings)

**If all 4 are No:** You may have a demand problem. Run a 30-day content test before building a paid tier.

**If 2+ are Yes:** You have demand. You have a packaging/CTA/tier problem. Continue.

---

## Step 2: Identify Your Type

### Type A Diagnosis — Packaging Gap

*Test:* Show your paid product page to someone who doesn't know your work. Ask: "What do you get if you buy this?" If they can't answer in 10 seconds, you have a packaging gap.

Symptoms:
- People view the paid page but don't buy
- You get "what's included?" questions
- Your description focuses on features, not outcomes
- The free and paid tiers feel like the same thing at different prices

Fix:
- Rewrite the paid tier description around a specific outcome, not a feature list
- Add one concrete deliverable that's unambiguously different from the free tier
- Lead with the buyer's problem, not your solution

**Example:** 
- ❌ "Pro tier includes all templates plus extras"
- ✅ "I review your specific repo and tell you exactly which governance gaps will cause problems first"

---

### Type B Diagnosis — CTA Gap

*Test:* Read your most-viewed free content from top to bottom. Count the number of times a paid offer is mentioned. If the answer is 0 or 1 (buried at the bottom), you have a CTA gap.

Symptoms:
- High free content views, zero paid page visits
- No referral traffic from free content to paid page in your analytics
- Your paid offer lives on a separate page with no path from your free content

Fix:
- Add a CTA to every piece of free content — minimum once, ideally twice (mid-content and end)
- The CTA must be specific: not "check out my paid tier" but "need implementation help? $199 — 1hr async consult"
- The CTA link must go directly to the purchase page, not a homepage

---

### Type C Diagnosis — Tier Mismatch

*Test:* Describe your paid tier buyer. Now describe who is actually consuming your free content. If they're different people, you have a tier mismatch.

Symptoms:
- CTAs exist but don't convert
- Your paid tier is priced for enterprise but your free audience is indie/solo
- Or: Your paid tier is priced at $5 but your audience expects and can afford $199+
- You're selling a $1,500 retainer from a free checklist with no bridge tier

Fix:
- Add a bridge tier that matches your free audience's budget and intent
- Pricing ladder: free → $199 (async consult) → $499/mo (bundle) → $1,500/mo (retainer)
- Never upsell more than one tier at a time from a free template

## Step 3: The Revenue Math

Run these numbers before building anything new.

```
Monthly free content views:       ___
Expected CTA click-through rate:  1-3% (conservative)
Expected CTA-to-purchase rate:    10-20% (warm audience)

Projected monthly buyers:
  [views] × 0.02 × 0.15 = ___

At $199/conversion:
  [buyers] × $199 = $___/month

Break-even buyer count for $1K/month:
  $1,000 / $199 = ~5 buyers/month
  Required views at 0.3% end-to-end conversion: ~1,667 views/month
```

**Implication:** If you have 11K monthly views and $0 revenue, you have a CTA or packaging problem — not a scale problem. 11K views at a 0.3% end-to-end conversion rate = 33 buyers/month = $6,567/month at $199. The gap between $0 and $6,567 is entirely structural.

---

## Step 4: 7-Day Fix Protocol

Do exactly these things in order. Do not skip ahead.

**Day 1-2 (Type B fix):**
- Add CTA to your 3 highest-traffic free templates
- Verify CTA link resolves
- Record baseline: paid page views before the CTA change

**Day 3-4 (Type A fix):**
- Rewrite your paid page description around one specific outcome
- Add one concrete deliverable that's unambiguously different from free tier

**Day 5-6 (Type C fix if needed):**
- If your paid tier is $500+, add a $99-$199 entry point
- The entry point should be low-effort for you: async audit, template pack, 1hr consult

**Day 7:**
- Check paid page views — did the CTA fix drive any traffic?
- Check conversion — did any of that traffic convert?
- If traffic went up but conversion stayed at 0: Type A problem — keep fixing packaging
- If traffic is still 0: CTA placement isn't working — move it earlier in the free content
