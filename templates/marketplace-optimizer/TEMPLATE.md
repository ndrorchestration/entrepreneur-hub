## The Core Problem

Most template creators have a distribution problem disguised as a demand problem. High views, low conversion is almost never about the template quality — it's about four specific structural gaps:

1. **Audience mismatch** — the template reaches the wrong buyer segment
2. **CTA absence** — no clear upgrade or purchase path from free content
3. **Packaging gap** — the paid tier isn't obviously differentiated from the free tier
4. **Platform fragmentation** — traffic is split across platforms with no cross-link strategy

This template gives you a systematic audit for all four.

## Diagnostic: Where Are You Breaking?

Answer each question. The first "No" you hit is your highest-leverage fix.

### Layer 1: Audience Fit
- [ ] Does your most-viewed template match the buyer profile of your paid tier?
- [ ] Is the same audience consuming your free content AND capable of buying your paid tier?
- [ ] Have you validated this with at least one real buyer conversation?

**If No on any:** Your distribution is generating the wrong traffic. Fix audience fit before optimizing anything else.

### Layer 2: CTA Presence
- [ ] Every published free template ends with a specific, actionable CTA
- [ ] The CTA link resolves and leads directly to a purchasable product (not a homepage)
- [ ] The CTA appears before the end of the template (not buried in footnotes)
- [ ] The CTA offer matches the reader's demonstrated interest

**If No on any:** You have traffic with no exit ramp to revenue. This is the fastest fix — no new content required.

### Layer 3: Paid Tier Clarity
- [ ] A buyer landing on your paid product page can answer "what do I get?" in under 10 seconds
- [ ] The paid tier is clearly differentiated from the free tier by outcome, not just quantity
- [ ] The price is visible without clicking anything
- [ ] There is exactly one primary purchase action on the page

**If No on any:** Your packaging is the blocker. People want to buy but can't figure out how or why.

### Layer 4: Cross-Platform Linkage
- [ ] Your highest-traffic free content links to your paid product
- [ ] Your paid product page links back to your free content (trust building)
- [ ] Your GitHub / portfolio links to your Needle / Gumroad storefront
- [ ] Your Needle storefront links to your GitHub (depth signal for buyers)

**If No on any:** Your traffic pools are isolated. Fix the links.

## The Four Marketplace Levers

### Lever 1: Template Title Optimization

Marketplace search is keyword-driven. Your title is the primary ranking signal.

**Formula:** `[Outcome] + [For Whom] + [Method/Framework]`

Examples:
- ❌ "AI Governance Pack" — vague, no audience signal
- ✅ "AI Governance Starter Pack for Entrepreneurs — DGAF Framework" — outcome + audience + method
- ❌ "Sprint Planner" — no AI signal, no audience signal
- ✅ "Solo AI Builder Sprint Planner — 2-Week Shipping Framework" — specific audience + specific outcome

### Lever 2: Description First Paragraph

The first 150 characters of your description appear in marketplace cards. Most creators waste this space on context. Buyers need outcome.

**Formula:** `[Who it's for]: [specific problem it solves] in [specific time/effort]`

Example: *"For AI founders: a complete governance kit that turns agent chaos into an auditable system in 45 minutes."*

### Lever 3: Free-to-Paid Upgrade Path

The conversion path must be:
- Visible in the free template itself (not just on your profile page)
- Specific about what the paid tier adds ("I'll review YOUR repo" not "get more")
- Priced at the right tier for the template's demonstrated audience (don't upsell a $1,500 retainer from a free checklist — bridge to $199 first)

### Lever 4: Social Proof Architecture

Buyers need a trust signal before they pay. For template creators without reviews yet:
- View count is a trust signal — display it ("11K+ views")
- Framework provenance is a trust signal — cite your methodology
- Use case specificity is a trust signal — "built for solo AI founders" beats "for everyone"
- GitHub depth is a trust signal — link your repo, it shows you're real

- [ ] Update view baseline in your tracking doc
